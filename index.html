Electronic Visa (eVisa)

Fast and convenient online visa application for eligible travelers

Tourist eVisa

Duration: 30 days stay
Validity: 90 days from issue
Processing: 2-5 business days
Required Documents:
  • Valid passport (6+ months)
  • Passport photo
  • Flight tickets
  • Accommodation proof

Duration: 30 days stay
Validity: 90 days from issue
Processing: 2-5 business days

  • Passport photo
  • Invitation letter
  • Bank statements
  • Travel itinerary

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about traveling to Eswatini

The best time to visit Eswatini is during the dry season from May to September when wildlife viewing is excellent and the weather is pleasant. The Reed Dance ceremony occurs in August/September, making it an ideal cultural experience time.

Yes, Eswatini is generally very safe for tourists. It's one of Africa's most peaceful kingdoms with low crime rates. However, we recommend standard travel precautions and staying with reputable tour operators.

Eswatini uses the Lilangeni (SZL), which is pegged 1:1 with the South African Rand (ZAR). South African Rand is widely accepted throughout the country. Credit cards are accepted at major establishments.

Yellow fever vaccination is required if arriving from a yellow fever endemic area. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended for certain regions. Consult your doctor or travel clinic 4-6 weeks before travel for personalized advice.

The official languages are English and siSwati. English is widely spoken in tourism, business, and government, making communication easy for international visitors.

The main airport is King Mswati III International Airport (SHO). Most international visitors fly into OR Tambo in Johannesburg, South Africa, then take a connecting flight or drive (3-4 hours). We can arrange transfers from South Africa.
